1. Criminal Law
What is Criminal Law?
Criminal Law deals with crimes, punishments, and justice systems. It focuses on protecting society by defining offenses and ensuring fair trials.
What you study in Criminal Law
Indian Penal Code (IPC) and Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC)
Students learn about crimes, punishments, and legal procedures.
Evidence Act and Case Laws
This subject teaches how evidence is evaluated in courts.
Skills you gain
Legal reasoning and argument skills
Students learn to analyze cases and build strong arguments.
Investigation and analytical skills
They understand legal procedures and evidence evaluation.
Career options
Criminal Lawyer / Public Prosecutor
They represent clients in criminal courts.
Judicial Services and Law Enforcement roles
Graduates work in judiciary-related positions.
Expected Salary
Freshers: ₹3–6 LPA
Experienced lawyers: ₹10–20 LPA
Higher Studies
LLM, Judicial Services, PhD
Future scope
Strong scope due to constant legal and justice system needs.
Who should choose?
Students interested in justice, law, and courtroom practice.

2. Corporate Law
What is Corporate Law?
Corporate Law deals with the legal rules and regulations governing companies, businesses, and corporate organizations. It focuses on how companies are formed, managed, regulated, and dissolved, and ensures that businesses operate within the legal framework. Corporate Law plays a crucial role in maintaining transparency, accountability, and ethical practices in the business world.
What you study in Corporate Law
Company Law and Corporate Governance
Students study how companies are incorporated, managed, and controlled. This subject explains the roles and responsibilities of directors, shareholders, and management in a company.
Business Laws and Commercial Contracts
This area focuses on legal agreements, contracts, mergers, and acquisitions. Students learn how legal documentation protects business interests and resolves disputes.
Securities Law and Corporate Compliance
Students understand stock markets, regulatory authorities, and compliance requirements. This subject ensures companies follow laws related to finance and investments.
Skills you gain
Legal drafting and documentation skills
Corporate Law trains students to prepare contracts, agreements, and legal documents with accuracy and clarity.
Analytical and advisory skills
Students learn to analyze legal risks and provide legal advice to businesses and organizations.
Career options after Corporate Law
Corporate Lawyer / Legal Advisor
They advise companies on legal matters, contracts, and compliance issues.
In-house Counsel for Corporations
They work within companies to handle legal operations and regulatory requirements.
Law Firms and Consultancy roles
Graduates work in corporate law firms, consulting firms, and multinational organizations.
Expected Salary (India)
Freshers: ₹5–8 LPA
With experience: ₹12–25 LPA
Top law firms and MNC roles: ₹30+ LPA
Higher Studies options
LLM in Corporate Law, MBA (Business + Law), PhD
Future scope of Corporate Law
The demand for corporate lawyers is continuously growing due to globalization, startups, and expanding business regulations.
Who should choose Corporate Law?
Students interested in business, legal strategy, corporate environments, and advisory roles should choose this specialization.
